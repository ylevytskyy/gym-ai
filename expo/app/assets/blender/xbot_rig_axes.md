## Translation (location) channels

`loc_X`, `loc_Y`, `loc_Z` on a pose bone translate the bone along its OWN LOCAL axes — **not** along world X/Y/Z. This is the most common gotcha: a beginner reaches for `loc_Z` to move the character up/down, but on the Hips bone the Z local axis may point world-forward, not world-up.

Mapping derived from this rig (rest pose, armature world matrix = identity):

| Bone | loc_X moves body in | loc_Y moves body in | loc_Z moves body in |
|------|---------------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| `Hips` | +X (lateral right) | +Z (up) | -Y (forward) |
| `Spine` | +X (lateral right) | +Z (up) | -Y (forward) |
| `Spine1` | +X (lateral right) | +Z (up) | -Y (forward) |
| `Spine2` | +X (lateral right) | +Z (up) | -Y (forward) |

> **Rule:** before writing any `loc_*` value, look up the bone row above and identify which local axis maps to world `+Z (up)`. That is your vertical-bounce channel.

> **Hips vertical bounce:** use `loc_Y` (not the other two). `loc_Y` is the world-up axis on this rig.

## Rotation channels

**Important caveat:** twist vs swing depends on the bone's CURRENT pose, not just rest pose. When a bone is first rotated into a working position (e.g. the arm bone rotated X+90° to hang at the side), ALL its local axes rotate with it, so what was the twist axis at rest may become the swing axis in the working pose. The rest-pose table above shows where local axes point at T-pose; in practice always verify with `--frame <peak>` for any bone that has a baseline rotation before animation begins.

The geometric rule: if a local axis aligns with the bone's own length (head→tail) at the CURRENT pose, rotating around it is a TWIST. The other two produce swing. Below are three examples using rest-pose geometry (valid for Spine and leg bones that animate near rest; arm bones have a baseline X+90° offset, see note above).

Three concrete examples from this rig (rest-pose geometry):

### `LeftUpLeg` (rest-pose analysis valid)

Bone points -Z (down) at rest — this is also the working pose.

- **Local Y** (-Z (down)) — parallel to bone length → **TWIST around bone's length.** Spins in place; does not swing the bone direction.
- **Local X** (-X (lateral left)) — perpendicular to bone → **sweep / flexion axis.** Swings the bone direction.
- **Local Z** (-Y (forward)) — perpendicular to bone → **sweep / flexion axis.** Swings the bone direction.

### `Spine` (rest-pose analysis valid)

Bone points +Z (up) at rest — this is also the working pose.

- **Local Y** (+Z (up)) — parallel to bone length → **TWIST around bone's length.** Spins in place; does not swing the bone direction.
- **Local X** (+X (lateral right)) — perpendicular to bone → **sweep / flexion axis.** Swings the bone direction.
- **Local Z** (-Y (forward)) — perpendicular to bone → **sweep / flexion axis.** Swings the bone direction.

### `LeftArm` (working-pose analysis — rest-pose geometry is misleading here)

At T-pose rest, the bone points +X (lateral right) and local Y also aligns with the bone (+X lateral right), which would geometrically identify Y as twist. **But** the arm is always first rotated X+90° to hang at the character's side before any animation begins. In that working position the arm points world -Z (down), and all local axes have rotated 90° with it:

- **Local Y** — in working pose (arm at side): **forward/back sagittal swing axis.** Verified empirically: Y-45° moves the forearm forward (world -Y direction). This is the running-arm swing axis used by `high_knees.py`.
- **Local Z** — in working pose (arm at side): **TWIST around bone length** (arm now points -Z; Z becomes the bone-length axis). Produces outward forearm sweep — NOT sagittal swing. Avoid for swing motion.
- **Local X** — abduction/adduction (arm swings toward/away from the body midline in the frontal plane). Used as the T-pose-to-at-side baseline (X+90° = arm hangs down).

### `LeftForeArm` (working-pose analysis — DIFFERENT from LeftArm despite same rest-pose geometry)

At rest, `LeftForeArm` has the same axis layout as `LeftArm` (+X lateral right, Y parallel to bone = twist at rest). However, its working-pose axes are **not the same** as LeftArm's working pose. Empirical test (introspection script, dot products at all shoulder Y values): **Y remains the TWIST axis at 1.000 dot throughout** — the forearm's local Y stays parallel to the bone direction regardless of how the shoulder swings. This is because the forearm axis system tracks the parent bone's rotation differently from LeftArm.

**LeftForeArm working-pose axes (verified at shoulder Y=-45°, 0°, +20°):**

- **Local Y** — TWIST axis in ALL working poses. Dot with bone direction = 1.000. Setting Y to any value produces **zero elbow angle change** — the forearm just spins on its own axis. This is the bug in the original `high_knees.py` (using Y for elbow flex).
- **Local Z** — **sagittal elbow flex axis.** Dot with bone direction = 0.000 (fully perpendicular). A ±90° rotation on Z produces exactly 90° world elbow angle change.
- **Local X** — also perpendicular (dot=0.000), also a flex axis, but crosses into the frontal plane — prefer Z.

**Sign conventions for Z flex — OPPOSITE between left and right (matching shoulder Y convention):**

| Bone | Z sign | Effect |
|------|--------|--------|
| `LeftForeArm`  | **Z+** | forearm folds **toward face** (forward/elbow-flex direction) |
| `LeftForeArm`  | **Z−** | forearm folds **away from face** |
| `RightForeArm` | **Z−** | forearm folds **toward face** (forward/elbow-flex direction) |
| `RightForeArm` | **Z+** | forearm folds **away from face** |

**Shoulder Y convention — also OPPOSITE between left and right (RightArm bone points -X):**

| Bone | Y sign | Effect |
|------|--------|--------|
| `LeftArm`  | **Y−** | arm swings **forward** |
| `LeftArm`  | **Y+** | arm swings **backward** |
| `RightArm` | **Y+** | arm swings **forward** (OPPOSITE of LeftArm — bone points -X) |
| `RightArm` | **Y−** | arm swings **backward** (OPPOSITE of LeftArm) |

**Critical insight (verified 2026-05-06):** The elbow always folds in the toward-face direction
(Z+ left / Z- right) whether the shoulder is forward or backward. When the shoulder swings back,
the upper arm tilts rearward, so the elbow fold still reads "toward face" but the hand ends up
at hip level behind the body because the whole upper arm chain is rotated backward. The forearm
Z sign does NOT flip between forward and back peaks.

**Full-pose world hand positions (bone HEAD, introspection 2026-05-06, includes spine+hips):**
- Left  fwd peak  (Z=+120°, LeftArm Y=-45°): hand Y=-0.249, Z=+1.537 (chin)
- Left  back peak (Z= +55°, LeftArm Y=+20°): hand Y=+0.006, Z=+1.009 (hip) ← Z+ not Z-
- Right fwd peak  (Z=-120°, RightArm Y=+45°): hand Y=-0.249, Z=+1.537 (chin)
- Right back peak (Z= -55°, RightArm Y=-20°): hand Y=+0.006, Z=+1.009 (hip) ← Z- not Z+
- Neutral (shoulder Y=0°): Left Z=+90°, Right Z=-90° → hand forward at Z≈1.16m

> **Rule of thumb:** for spine and leg bones that animate near T-pose, read the "Bone points" column — the local axis that matches it is TWIST, the other two sweep. For **LeftArm/RightArm** with baseline X+90° offset: **Y = sagittal swing** (Y- fwd / Y+ back for Left; Y+ fwd / Y- back for Right), **Z = twist, X = abduction**. For **LeftForeArm/RightForeArm**: **Y = TWIST (always), Z = sagittal flex** — signs mirror the arm's Y convention: Z+ toward face for Left, Z- toward face for Right. Always verify with full pose + bone HEAD position; isolated-bone and tail-position readings differ from the full-pose bone-HEAD values used by validators.
